Biography

A Career Built on
One Commitment.

Cliff Venable is a criminal defense attorney who has spent his entire legal career successfully defending people accused of crimes in Salt Lake County and the surrounding area. His commitment to his clients is defined by years of experience defending some of the most challenging and complex criminal cases in Utah.

From obtaining not guilty verdicts for clients charged with aggravated assault, aggravated robbery, DUI, and domestic violence — to convincing prosecutors to move for dismissal in narcotics distribution and assault cases — Cliff relentlessly champions his clients' rights at every stage.

Cliff understands that facing criminal charges can be an incredibly stressful and overwhelming experience. He approaches each case with empathy and directness, ensuring that clients feel supported and informed throughout the legal process. By maintaining open lines of communication, he ensures that his clients are well-prepared and confident as their cases progress.

Having practiced in Salt Lake City and surrounding jurisdictions for more than 15 years, Cliff has developed strong working relationships within the local legal community — including with judges, prosecutors, and court staff. That familiarity with the local court system allows Salt City Defense to craft legal strategies that align with the nuances of each courtroom and case.

The Approach

How Cliff Works.

1
Trial-Ready from Day One

Every case is approached with the expectation it could go to trial. That standard of preparation — thorough investigation, early motion practice, detailed case theory — is what forces better outcomes at every stage before trial becomes necessary.

2
Proactive Defense Planning

Cliff analyzes potential outcomes and builds contingency plans that account for every possible scenario — so when the case moves, you're never caught unprepared.

3
Skilled Negotiation

Years of experience negotiating with Utah prosecutors allow Cliff to identify the leverage points in a case and secure favorable outcomes — dismissed charges, reduced charges, or alternative sentencing — before trial becomes necessary.

4
Clear, Consistent Communication

Clients receive regular updates, honest assessments of where their case stands, and timely responses to questions. Facing criminal charges is already overwhelming — not knowing what's happening with your case makes it worse.

5
Focused on Your Outcome

Whether the goal is to avoid jail time, reduce charges, secure a dismissal, or address post-conviction matters like expungements and probation modifications — every decision is made in service of the outcome you need.
